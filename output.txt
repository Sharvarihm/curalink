question:what are the symptoms and treatment for lung cancer
response:
 Overview
Lung cancer symptoms can vary depending on the stage and type of cancer, but common symptoms include coughing, chest pain, and shortness of breath [PUB1]. The treatment for lung cancer depends on the stage and type of cancer, with options including surgery, radiation, chemotherapy, immunotherapy, or molecularly targeted therapy [PUB4]. Early diagnosis and treatment are crucial to improve overall survival rates.

📚 Research Findings
Symptoms and Physical Signs: The symptoms and physical signs of lung cancer were associated with the stage and pathological diagnosis of non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC), with patients with squamous carcinoma being more likely to develop symptoms [PUB1].
Treatment Options: Current treatments for NSCLC include chemotherapy, chemoradiotherapy, targeted therapy, antiangiogenic therapy, immunotherapy, and combination therapy [PUB3].
Emotional Distress: Emotional distress, such as anxiety and depression, can impact treatment adherence and overall survival in lung cancer patients [PUB3].
Nanomedicine Delivery: A novel nanodrug delivery system, SPIO@SiO-ANB, has been developed to enhance the treatment of NSCLC by combining ferroptosis induction with targeted therapy [PUB4].
Lung Cancer Screening: Lung cancer screening is crucial for early detection and treatment, with low-dose computed tomography (LDCT) being a recommended screening method for high-risk individuals [PUB11].

question:“Latest treatment for lung cancer”
response:
 Overview
The latest treatment for lung cancer includes a range of options such as surgery, radiation, chemotherapy, immunotherapy, or molecularly targeted therapy [PUB1]. Recent studies have shown that nivolumab plus ipilimumab can increase 5-year survivorship compared to chemotherapy in patients with metastatic non-small cell lung cancer [PUB12]. Additionally, domvanalimab combined with zimberelimab has shown promising results as a first-line treatment for patients with PD-L1-high, advanced non-small cell lung cancer [PUB4].

📚 Research Findings
Current Treatments: Current treatments for non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC) include chemotherapy, chemoradiotherapy, targeted therapy, antiangiogenic therapy, immunotherapy, and combination therapy [PUB3].
Emerging Evidence: The personalized treatment paradigm for NSCLC has evolved significantly, with promising agents shifting the treatment paradigm across all stages [PUB10].
MEK Inhibitors: MEK inhibitors have gained approval for the treatment of patients with NSCLC and are being investigated in combination with other therapies [PUB11].
Sex-Based Differences: Research has identified sex-based differences in the effectiveness of immune checkpoint inhibitors in the treatment of advanced non-small cell lung cancer [PUB6].
Neoadjuvant Immunotherapy: Neoadjuvant immunotherapy has shown potential in improving treatment outcomes for stage III non-small cell lung cancer [TRIAL6].
Targeted Therapies: Targeted therapies, such as osimertinib, have been approved for the treatment of patients with mutated epidermal growth factor receptor non-small cell lung cancer [TRIAL4].
